## Scope

Use for:

- improving paragraph-to-paragraph flow in drafts that already have solid structure
- reducing abrupt jumps and improving signposting for skimmers

Do not use for:

- fixing unclear argument logic
- replacing weak ideas with connective phrases

### Preview Structure

- Here is what we will cover...
- This guide walks you through...
- First, let us look at...
- We will start with X, then move to Y...

### Introduce New Topics

- Now let us talk about...
- Another key factor is...
- X is worth exploring because...

### Refer Back

- As mentioned earlier...
- Building on that point...
- Going back to X...

### Move Between Sections

- Next up...
- With that covered, let us turn to...
- That brings us to...

### Addition

- Also...
- On top of that...
- Beyond that...
- There is also...

### Contrast

- However...
- That said...
- In contrast...
- While X is true, Y...

### Similarity

- Similarly...
- Likewise...
- In the same way...

### Cause and Effect

- As a result...
- That is why...
- This leads to...
- Here is what happens...

### Examples

- For example...
- Consider this...
- To illustrate...
- Say you want to...

### Emphasis

- Here is the key takeaway...
- What matters most is...
- The bottom line...

### Evidence

- According to [source]...
- Data from [source] indicates...
- A study by [source] found...
- Evidence suggests...

### Summaries and Conclusions

- To recap...
- In short...
- Let us pull this together...
- Here is what to do next...

### Question-Based Transitions

- So what does this mean for you?
- Why does this matter?
- What is the catch?
- Wondering where to start?
